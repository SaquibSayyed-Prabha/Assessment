Q1. What is the purpose of head()? 

> head() displays the first 5 rows of a DataFrame by default. It is used to quickly preview the data.


Q2. What is the purpose of tail()? 

> tail() displays the last 5 rows of a DataFrame by default. It is used to view the ending records of 
the dataset.


Q3. What does shape return? 

> shape returns a tuple containing the number of rows and columns in the DataFrame in the format 
(rows, columns).


Q4. What does info() display? 

> info() displays a summary of the DataFrame, including the number of rows, column names, data types,
non-null values, and memory usage.


Q5. What does describe() display? 

> describe() displays statistical information about numerical columns, such as count, mean, standard 
deviation, minimum, maximum, and quartile values.
